📝 How FCE Keyword Transformation Works

Complete the second sentence so that it has a similar meaning to the first sentence, using the word given. Do not change the word given. You must use between two and five words, including the word given.

Important: "Do not change the word" means you cannot replace it with a different word. However, you must change its form (tense, person, number) to fit the sentence. For example:

  • Keyword: GIVE → Answer: gave up (changed tense from present to past)
  • Keyword: BELIEVE → Answer: is believed to be (changed form to passive)
  • Keyword: IMPORTANT → Answer: is important to (changed structure, same word)

🔗 Essential Phrasal Verbs for FCE

These phrasal verbs appear frequently in Cambridge English exams and are crucial for B2 level proficiency.

Category 1: Negative/Difficult Situations

GIVE UP

Stop doing something/surrender

"She gave up smoking last year."
PUT UP WITH

Tolerate/endure something unpleasant

"I won't put up with his rudeness."
PUT OFF

Postpone/delay

"Don't put off studying for the exam."
BREAK DOWN

Stop working/fail/have an emotional crisis

"The car broke down on the way."
FALL APART

Disintegrate/deteriorate

"His business is falling apart."
BRING DOWN

Reduce/make someone sad

"The bad news brought down his spirits."

Category 2: Discovery/Investigation

FIND OUT

Discover/learn something

"I found out the truth yesterday."
LOOK INTO

Investigate/examine closely

"The police are looking into the crime."
WORK OUT

Calculate/understand/develop

"Can you work out the answer?"
FIGURE OUT

Understand/solve

"It took time to figure out the problem."
COME ACROSS

Find by chance/encounter

"I came across an old letter."
DIG OUT

Search for and find

"She dug out the old photos."

Category 3: Positive Action/Progress

TAKE UP

Start a hobby/occupy time or space

"He took up painting after retirement."
CARRY ON

Continue/persevere

"Please carry on with your work."
MOVE FORWARD

Progress/advance

"Let's move forward with the project."
GET AHEAD

Make progress/succeed

"She got ahead through hard work."
BUILD UP

Increase gradually/strengthen

"He built up his confidence slowly."
BRING ABOUT

Cause to happen

"Technology brought about major changes."

Category 4: Relationships & Communication

GET ON WITH

Have a good relationship with

"She gets on well with her colleagues."
FALL OUT WITH

Have a disagreement/end friendship

"They fell out over money."
CATCH UP WITH

Meet/reach someone ahead

"Wait for me, I'll catch up with you."
MAKE UP

Reconcile/compensate

"They made up after their argument."
BRING UP

Raise/mention a topic

"He brought up an interesting point."
SPEAK UP

Express opinion/voice concern

"She spoke up for the victims."

Category 5: Physical Actions & States

TURN UP

Appear/arrive

"He turned up late again."
TURN DOWN

Reject/reduce volume

"She turned down the job offer."
PICK UP

Collect/lift/learn quickly

"Can you pick me up at 5?"
PULL OFF

Succeed in doing something difficult

"He managed to pull off the deal."
CUT DOWN

Reduce/limit

"I'm trying to cut down on sugar."
SET UP

Establish/arrange

"She set up her own business."

🏗️ Most Common FCE Word Formation Structures

These are the grammatical structures that appear most frequently in Part 4 (Key Word Transformation) of the FCE exam.

Passive Voice Transformations

Active → Passive (Simple)

Pattern: Active sentence requires passive transformation

❌ Active: They make computers here.
✅ Passive: Computers are made here.

Passive: Be + Past Participle

Pattern: Transform to different tense passive

❌ They will announce the results.
✅ The results will be announced.

Passive with "Get"

Pattern: Replace "be" with "get"

❌ She was caught in the rain.
✅ She got caught in the rain.

Modal & Semi-Modal Transformations

Ability: Can → Be Able To

Pattern: Express ability differently

❌ He can't swim.
✅ He is not able to swim.

Obligation: Must → Have To

Pattern: Express obligation differently

❌ You must finish your homework.
✅ You have to finish your homework.

Possibility: May/Might

Pattern: Express possibility differently

❌ Perhaps he will come.
✅ He might come.

Conditional Structures

First Conditional

Pattern: If + present, will (real future)

❌ If you're late, we'll start without you.
✅ If you're late, we'll start without you.

Second Conditional

Pattern: If + past, would (imaginary)

❌ If he called, what would you say?
✅ If he called, what would you say?

Third Conditional

Pattern: If + past perfect, would have (regret)

❌ I didn't know, so I didn't call.
✅ If I had known, I would have called.

Comparative & Superlative Structures

Comparative: More/Less

Pattern: Compare two things

❌ He is smarter than his brother.
✅ He is more intelligent than his brother.

The More...The More

Pattern: Cause and effect

❌ As you study more, you improve.
✅ The more you study, the more you improve.

Superlative Form

Pattern: Highest degree

❌ No student is as intelligent as Maria.
✅ Maria is the most intelligent student.

Infinitive & Gerund Structures

To-Infinitive

Pattern: After certain verbs/adjectives

❌ She wants swimming.
✅ She wants to swim.

Managed/Succeeded To Do

Pattern: Past achievement

❌ He could finish on time.
✅ He managed to finish on time.

Subject + Infinitive

Pattern: Infinitive as subject

❌ That you arrive early is important.
✅ It is important to arrive early.

Relative Clauses & Cleft Sentences

Relative Pronouns (Who/Which)

Pattern: Additional information

❌ The girl was clever. She won.
✅ The girl who won was clever.

Cleft Sentences (What/It)

Pattern: Emphasis

❌ His honesty surprised me most.
✅ What surprised me was his honesty.

Relative Adverbs (Where/When)

Pattern: Time/place/reason

❌ I saw her there.
✅ That's the place where I saw her.
